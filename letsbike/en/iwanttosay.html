# A Note from the Developer I am a **Christian**, an **independent software developer**, and a father of three (plus a cat). In our family, I see myself primarily as a caregiver. I don’t want traditional role expectations—where one works outside and the other manages everything at home—to quietly define how we live. Because of this, I rarely have the time to go to the gym or set aside long stretches for running. Amid this busy rhythm of life, my main mode of transportation—my scooter—suddenly broke down. While considering buying a new one, an alternative came to mind: cycling. Looking back at how I used my scooter: 1. I rarely washed it, only wiping it down after rainy days. 2. I did minimal maintenance—just routine replacements (engine oil, gear oil, tires, air filter). 3. Most of my transportation relied on public transit or taxis; for temporary needs, I would rent a car or scooter. 4. I almost never rode for leisure. A long-distance ride during my student years confirmed that I’m not particularly fond of scooter trips. 5. I have a fairly clear understanding of my own habits and needs. That reflection led me to plan and develop this app. --- ## Development Journey This is the second time I’ve built an application from scratch. - I didn’t start with a mind map, assuming it would just be a small utility—only to realize the feature set kept expanding. - Through UI/UX design, I translated my ideal workflow into something tangible. - With the help of AI, I chose to develop it natively to ensure better performance and user experience. During development, I realized that some core concepts needed clearer definition and that data sources had to be more thoroughly verified. Fortunately, Taiwan provides comprehensive and well-maintained open data resources, making it possible to find nearly everything required. --- ## Future Plans If given the opportunity to receive official certification, I hope to integrate official features and carbon-reduction incentive systems to create a smoother and more accurate workflow. Departure and arrival stations could then be retrieved directly from verified sources, reducing estimation and improving precision. Until then, this app will continue to focus primarily on meeting my own practical needs. If you have ideas or suggestions, feel free to reach out. Your feedback may inspire the next feature—or help refine the experience even further.
