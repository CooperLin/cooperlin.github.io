# A Note from the Developer I'm a **Christian**, an **indie software developer**, and a dad of three kids (plus one cat). When it comes to my role at home, I see myself primarily as a caregiver — I never wanted the traditional "breadwinner husband, homemaker wife" dynamic to quietly take root in our family. I firmly believe: 1. The percentage of household chores a husband takes on **directly equals** the percentage of happiness in the home. 2. Happy Wife, Happy Life. So instead of scheduling things that would leave all three kids with my wife, I look for pockets of time within our existing daily routine where I can squeeze in some exercise. It was within this kind of lifestyle that my main mode of transportation — my scooter — suddenly broke down. While thinking about whether to get a new one, another option came to mind: switching to a bicycle for my daily commute. Looking back at how I actually used my scooter: 1. Almost never washed it — just a quick wipe-down after rain. 2. Almost no proactive maintenance — only the basics (oil, gear oil, tires, air filter). 3. For family outings, we mostly use public transit or taxis; rental cars for one-off needs. 4. Almost never rode for leisure. Did one long ride back in my student days and confirmed that motorcycle touring just isn't my thing. 5. I know my own habits and needs pretty well. Taking stock of all this, a bicycle seemed like a genuinely viable option — but riding alone felt like it was still missing something. On top of commuting by bike, I also happen to be an **indie software developer** — maybe that's exactly what could fill the gap. So I started planning and building this app. --- ## Development Story This is the second app I've built from scratch. - I didn't start with a mind map — I thought it would be a small utility. It grew far beyond that. - Through UI/UX design, I turned the ideal usage flow in my head into something tangible. - With the help of AI, I committed to native development to ensure performance and a quality user experience. Along the way, I realized my understanding of certain core concepts wasn't as solid as I thought, and the data sources needed more careful organization. Fortunately, Taiwan has excellent public data and open information resources — nearly everything I needed had a reliable source. A plan, the data, and a real personal need — there was no reason not to build this. --- ## What's Next If official certification becomes available down the road, I'd love to integrate official features and mechanisms like a "carbon savings ledger," making the whole experience smoother and more accurate. Origin and destination stops could then pull directly from verified station data, reducing estimation and error. Until then, this app will continue to prioritize my own real-world usage needs. If you have any suggestions or ideas, feel free to reach out. Maybe the next update will bring a new feature — or a smoother experience — because of something you said.
